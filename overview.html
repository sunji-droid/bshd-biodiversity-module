Why This Training Module Exists

Botswana is experiencing biodiversity loss driven by changing climatic conditions, pollution, land use change, and overexploitation of resources, among other causes. This manifests as severe environmental degradation across our national landscapes—including the accelerated drying of rivers, shifting rainfall patterns leading to localized desertification, flash flooding, and deep ecological disruptions that directly threaten our unique biodiversity.

These compounding pressures have intensified human-wildlife conflict along rural and protected area boundaries, altered traditional wildlife migration corridors, diminished the fundamental capacity of nature to sustain agricultural and communal livelihoods, and driven localized human displacement. As ecosystems come under increasing strain, the need for accurate, investigative, and nuanced public communication has never been more urgent.

⚠️ The Critical Reporting Gap in Media Houses

Research and newsroom audits suggest that Botswana journalists are not currently adequately equipped to report deeply on biodiversity, and media houses lack sufficient technical and financial resources to consistently cover the subject beyond sporadic breaking events. This leaves biodiversity and systemic environmental degradation drastically under-reported in the public sphere. This specialized training module exists specifically to close that institutional and skill gap.

Module Structure & Learning Approach

Designed as an interactive, comprehensive digital toolkit, this module guides journalists, editors, and communication specialists from fundamental global biodiversity science right through to national policy domestication, investigative financial tracking, and solutions journalism. Whether reporting from Gaborone, Maun, Francistown, or remote community conservation areas along the Okavango and Chobe river systems, journalists can utilize these five dedicated units to generate impactful stories that hold power accountable and amplify sustainable solutions.
