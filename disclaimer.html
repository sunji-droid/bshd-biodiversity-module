Section 04 • Legal & Institutional Framework

Disclaimer & Terms of Use

Important Institutional Independence, Educational Purpose, and Editorial Guidelines.

⚖️ Status: Institutional Review Pending (Draft Legal Text)

⚖️ PENDING LEGAL & INSTITUTIONAL REVIEW — DRAFT DISCLAIMER

This page contains standard professional legal and educational disclaimers appropriate for a donor-funded journalism training resource. It is clearly marked as a placeholder working draft pending formal legal and institutional review by the Botswana Society for Human Development (BSHD), ClimateFeatures, and Internews Network. Do not present or rely on this draft as final binding legal copy until approved by the project partners.

1. Attribution of Views & Independence

The findings, interpretations, viewpoints, conclusions, and editorial recommendations expressed throughout this training module—including all units, case studies, academic contributions, and external expert analyses—are solely those of their respective authors, curriculum leads, and academic contributors. They do not necessarily reflect, represent, or constitute the official policy, stance, or position of the Botswana Society for Human Development (BSHD), ClimateFeatures, or the Internews Network, nor their respective Boards of Directors, management, donors, or affiliated institutional partners.

2. Educational & Journalistic Purpose

This digital module is published strictly for educational, pedagogical, and capacity-building purposes to support working journalists, editors, media houses, and communication students across Botswana. While every effort has been made to ensure that ecological facts, legal summaries (such as the Convention on Biological Diversity or the National Biodiversity Strategy and Action Plan), and institutional mandates (including those of DWNP and DFRR) are accurate at the time of compilation, this module is not intended to serve as formal legal advice, official scientific certification, or prescriptive policy directives.

🔍 Verification Responsibility for Journalists

Journalists utilizing this module as a starting point for investigative or explanatory reporting maintain complete editorial independence and full professional responsibility for their publications. Reporters are strongly advised to independently verify data, consult primary scientific research, conduct field interviews, and cross-check official statistics with relevant regulatory bodies (such as BUAN, DWNP, DFRR, and Statistics Botswana) before publishing news stories or broadcasts.

3. External Links & Reference Materials

Throughout this training curriculum, references, external links, case study citations, and third-party data portals may be cited to assist journalists in locating deeper evidence (such as remote sensing tools, global databases, or peer-reviewed journals). BSHD, ClimateFeatures, and Internews Network do not endorse, guarantee the continuous accuracy of, or accept legal liability for the content of third-party websites or external publications referenced in this module.

4. Intellectual Property & Fair Use in Reporting

In accordance with the goal of strengthening public environmental discourse across Botswana, accredited journalists, media houses, and educational institutions are encouraged to utilize concepts, frameworks, and story evaluation tips from this module in their professional reporting. When directly quoting substantial portions of this curriculum or citing specific academic contributions (such as the resource economics analysis by Professor Fadhel Kabou), appropriate attribution must be provided to the respective author and this joint institutional module.

5. Unlisted Review Status Notice

During this Working Progress phase, this website is unlisted and carries mandatory search engine indexing restrictions (`noindex, nofollow` and root `robots.txt` disallow rules). Authorized reviewers and partners are requested not to distribute public URLs or excerpt unfinalized draft sections until BSHD officially confirms the conclusion of the review process and the public launch of the module.
