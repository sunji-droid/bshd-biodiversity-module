About Botswana Society for Human Development (BSHD)

[PLACEHOLDER INSTITUTIONAL DESCRIPTION — PENDING FINAL CLIENT REVIEW]: The Botswana Society for Human Development (BSHD) is a premier national civil society organization dedicated to advancing sustainable human development, youth empowerment, community resilience, and social justice across Botswana. Operating at the vital intersection of human wellbeing and environmental sustainability, BSHD recognizes that long-term human development is fundamentally impossible without a thriving, healthy natural ecosystem.

Under the guidance of Project Lead Sharon Tshipa, BSHD spearheads initiatives that empower Batswana to address pressing societal challenges, from climate change adaptation and livelihood security to democratic engagement and gender equality. Through tailored training programs, community mobilization, and evidence-based policy advocacy, BSHD bridges the gap between grassroots community needs and national policy implementation.

As the overseeing organization for this training module, BSHD is committed to strengthening the media ecosystem in Botswana. By providing journalists with the technical knowledge and analytical tools required to investigate biodiversity loss and environmental degradation, BSHD aims to foster a more informed citizenry, stimulate robust public discourse, and drive accountable governance over Botswana's precious natural resources.

Content & Technical Partner

About ClimateFeatures

[PLACEHOLDER INSTITUTIONAL DESCRIPTION — PENDING FINAL CLIENT REVIEW]: ClimateFeatures is a specialized environmental media and communications initiative dedicated to elevating rigorous, science-driven, and engaging journalism on climate change, biodiversity conservation, and ecological sustainability across African newsrooms.

Recognizing that environmental issues are often overshadowed by day-to-day political and economic headlines, ClimateFeatures partners with newsrooms, independent reporters, and academic institutions to develop innovative storytelling frameworks. Their work focuses on translating dense scientific data—such as IPBES global assessments, IPCC climate models, and national biodiversity action plans—into compelling narratives that resonate with local communities and policymakers alike.

In this collaborative module, ClimateFeatures brings extensive editorial expertise, pedagogical structuring, and thematic insight. By co-designing the curriculum, ClimateFeatures ensures that working journalists in Botswana gain mastery over specialized reporting methodologies, including data visualization, remote sensing verification, rights-based environmental framing, and constructive solutions journalism.

Supporting Partner & Donor

About Internews Network

[PLACEHOLDER INSTITUTIONAL DESCRIPTION — PENDING FINAL CLIENT REVIEW]: Internews Network is an international non-profit media development organization supporting independent journalism and access to trustworthy, high-quality information in over 100 countries worldwide. For over four decades, Internews has worked alongside local journalists, civil society advocates, and newsroom leaders to build resilient media ecosystems capable of reporting on critical public interest issues.

Through specialized initiatives such as its Earth Journalism Network (EJN), Internews has trained more than 15,000 environmental journalists globally, providing small reporting grants, mentoring, technical workshops, and cross-border collaborative frameworks. Internews recognizes that when local journalists have the capacity and resources to report accurately on environmental degradation, communities are empowered to hold institutions accountable, protect vulnerable habitats, and adapt sustainably to ecological shifts.

The support provided by Internews Network for this Botswana biodiversity reporting module reflects a shared commitment to strengthening local media independence, editorial excellence, and investigative depth across Southern Africa.
